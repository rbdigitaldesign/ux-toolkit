UX research methodologies

Usability testing

Observe how real students interact with your system to identify usability issues. Choose moderated or unmoderated formats depending on context.

Moderated: conducted live, often via Zoom or Teams.
Unmoderated: task-based with screen capture (e.g. Maze or Lookback).

Card sorting

Understand how users categorise information. Helps shape site navigation and labelling. Use digital tools like OptimalSort or physical index cards.

Tree testing

Assess how easily users can find items in a proposed site structure. Complements card sorting and validates IA decisions.

User interviews

Collect qualitative insights from students. Explore motivations, challenges, and expectations. Use semi-structured guides for balance and flexibility.

Focus groups

Facilitate structured discussions among small groups of students. Useful for exploring shared perceptions or surfacing divergent views.

Diary studies

Ask students to document their experience over time, often during course use or interaction with learning platforms. Yields rich longitudinal data.
